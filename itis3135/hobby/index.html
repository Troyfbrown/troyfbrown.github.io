About Bass Drums

Bass drums are the big sideways drums that you always see out on the marching band field (pictured below). They range from sizes of 16" in diameter to 32" in diameter each creating different pitches that get lower as the size increases.

The bass drum is played with a pair of mallets typically by 5-6 individuals. When all the individuals play in unison, they had the typical "bass" sound that we associate with music. But when playing independently, due to their variety of tones, they are melodic in nature. With each of the 5-6 individuals contributing one pitch that when put together with the others creates a melody.
